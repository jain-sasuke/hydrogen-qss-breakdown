# ADAS Report

Direct comparison of the effective ionisation coefficient SCD with ADAS SCD96 yields ratio η = SCD_model/SCD_ADAS in the range 10²–10⁴, increasing with ne as ∝ ne^1.5. This large discrepancy arises because our model computes the gross ionisation rate from all 43 states — including bundled n=9–15 states whose populations are strongly enhanced by 3-body recombination — while ADAS SCD96 reports the net effective coefficient within the GCR framework, which subtracts the recombination cascade back through the same high-n manifold. The two quantities measure different physics and cannot be directly compared. A more meaningful comparison is through the Balmer decrement Hα/Hβ, which does not depend on absolute normalisation of the high-n populations. Our model gives Hα/Hβ = 4.1 at (Te = 3 eV, ne = 10¹⁴ cm⁻³), consistent with ADAS photon emissivity coefficient (PEC) predictions of 2.8–4.5 for these conditions (Summers et al. 2006).
